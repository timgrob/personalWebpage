About Me

Hi, my name is David Tim Grob. I studied mechanical engineering at the Swiss Federal Institution of Technology in Zurich (ETH Zurich). During my undergdraduate degree I mainly focused on controlling and optimization. For my master degree I set my focuse on dynamic programming and machine learning.

I am currently reading for my doctoral degree at Oxford University. My reaserch deals with the separation of magnetic particles in microfluidic channels, which can be used for medical applications. The goal is to develop a diagnostic device, which is able to quickly, reliably and cheaply diagnose preeclampsia in an early stage. (Download my paper)

Even if I kind of got sidetracked during my PhD, I am still highly interested in optimization and machine learning. Therefore, I am about to expand my horizon in machine learning and dynamic programming again by reading Pattern Recognition And Machine Learning by Christopher M. Bishop.

However, I am not only interested in microtechnology and machine learing.Another topic I am highly passionate about is programming. I currently know MATLAB, JAVA, C++, HTML, CSS, JS, Python and also a little bit of Objective C, and I am still trying to dive into new programming languages or frameworks. I do truly care about beautiful code and I always embrace best practise.

If I am not coding, I enjoy sports, mostly running. I am a middle distance runner, where I also used to compete nationally. Unfortunately, a herniated disk made me stop competing. I still enjoy running but I no longer do it competitively anymore.
